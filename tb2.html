Troublemaker 2, Release date and demo

Originating from an ambitious project that continued to generate massive hype across various social media platforms and ultimately culminated in a substandard action game product, the Indonesian game - Troublemaker crafted by Gamecom Team still has plenty of room to grow and evolve. With strong signals that they will continue to expand the story and world they have created, it turns out we don't have to wait too long until a sequel series is voiced to the public. Leveraging the IGDX Game Showcase 2023 event, Gamecom officially announced the sequel series - Troublemaker 2: Beyond Dream.

Not only just a teaser, this announcement also comes with a debut trailer showcasing a world that is no longer "trapped" in the school atmosphere like its first series, as well as confirmation of the two protagonist system - Jordan and Budi. Melee combat action still appears to be a focal point along with its signature finishing animations, now also supported by more mini-games, including band performances. Gamecom Team also ensures that key characters from its first series will return in this sequel series.

Troublemaker 2: Beyond Dream itself is currently planned to be released in the upcoming year of 2025, with an exact release date yet to be determined.

Become an open world game

The game Troublemaker 2: Beyond Dream will feature a different game genre, namely becoming an open-world game. This sequel game will be set in a fictional world called South Jayakarta, a city depicted as a diverse city in Indonesia. With the concept of an open world, players can freely explore interesting places and discover some unexpected things.

There are 2 available characters that can be used.

In this sequel game, you actually have 2 available characters that can be used. So, you can play with 2 different characters, one as Jordan or a member of the band Beyond Dream, and the other as Budi, a member of the Parakacuk gang. Both of them will overcome all obstacles to pursue their goals.
